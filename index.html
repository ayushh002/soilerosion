SOIL EROSION

Soil erosion is the natural process by which soil, rock, and other Earth materials are displaced and transported from one location to another by the action of various agents, such as water, wind, ice, or gravity.

Erosion Due to Water

Water erosion is caused by the detachment and transport of soil by rainfall, runoff, melting snow or ice, and irrigation. Excessive erosion can threaten the production of agricultural and forest products. Erosion may also impact water conveyance and storage structures, and contribute to pollution from land surfaces.

Erosion Due to Wind

Wind erosion is a natural process that moves soil from one location to another by wind power. It can cause significant economic and environmental damage.

Climate Change

Efforts to combat soil erosion, such as afforestation, reforestation, improved land management practices, and soil conservation techniques, can help mitigate the climate change impacts associated with soil erosion. Additionally, reducing greenhouse gas emissions through sustainable agriculture and land use practices is essential to addressing the broader climate crisis.
